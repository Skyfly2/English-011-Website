Austin, TX

Austin

Population: 964,254
Median Home Price: $312,300
Median Rent: $1,225
Poverty Rate: 14.5%
Median Income: $67,462
Austin is the capital and fourth largest city in the southern state of Texas. Austin is one of the fastest growing tech cities in the world and it is home to the University of Texas At Austin, one of the best Computer Science schools in the world. The median software engineer in Austin makes about $110,000 per year—equivalent to about $195,000 in San Francisco.

Boston, MA

Boston

Population: 694,583
Median Home Price: $487,300
Median Rent: $1,539
Poverty Rate: 20.2%
Median Income: $65,883
Boston is the capital and largest city in the northeastern state of Massachussetts. Boston is also a fairly expensive city to live in, but it is one of the most historically important cities in the United States, is the home of a collection of world-renowned colleges and Universities, and boasts a lively tech scene.

Denver, CO

Denver

Population: 716,492
Median Home Price: $357,300
Median Rent: $1,217
Poverty Rate: 13.8%
Median Income: $63,793
Denver is the capital and most populated city in the state of Colorado. Denver is known as the "mile high city" because of its elevation of exactly one mile. The median software engineer in Denver makes roughly $116,000 per year—equivalent to about $180,000 in San Francisco.

Charlotte, NC

Charlotte

Population: 872,498
Median Home Price: $200,500
Median Rent: $1,086
Poverty Rate: 14.0%
Median Income: $60,886
Charlotte is the largest city in North Carolina and is currently one of the fastes growing cities in the United States. Charlotte boasts an active tech scene, plenty of things to do, a cheap housing market, and one of the youngest populations in the United States.

Seattle, WA

Seattle

Population: 744,955
Median Home Price: $605,200
Median Rent: $1,496
Poverty Rate: 11.8%
Median Income: $85,562
Seattle is the most populous city in Washington and generally considered as the second largest tech area in the United States. Seattle is home to plenty of start-ups as well as tech giants Amazon and Microsoft. The median Software Engineer takes home $125,000 which equates to about $160,000 in San Francisco due to its lower cost of living.

Los Angeles, CA

Los Angeles

Population: 3,990,456
Median Home Price: $599,700
Median Rent: $1,376
Poverty Rate: 19.1%
Median Income: $58,385
Los Angeles is the most populous city in California and resides south of the Bay Area. Known for its prominence in the film industry, celebrities, and good weather, west Los Angeles is also home to Silicon Beach; another area known for its start-ups and tech-scene. While still being considered an expensive city, the affordability of living in Los Angeles trumps that of Silicon Valley.

San Diego, CA

San Diego

Population: 1,425,976
Median Home Price: $569,100
Median Rent: $1,611
Poverty Rate: 13.8%
Median Income: $75,456
San Diego is a neighbor to the south of the Bay Area in California. San Diego is considered to be less expensive than the Bay Area, and While not the home of any major tech companies, it has a very large tech scene and many large tech companies have offices in San Diego.
